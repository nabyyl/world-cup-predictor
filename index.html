Scoring Rules

3 pts Exact score
1 pt Correct one team's score
1 pt Correct winner / draw / advanced team
1 pt Correct first team to score / no goal
10 pts Correct tournament winner
10 pts Correct best player
5 pts Each correct finalist

For each match, you can earn points from multiple areas. The maximum match points are 5 points: exact score, correct winner or advanced team, and correct first team to score or no-goal prediction.

Match prediction points are: 3 points for exact score, 1 point for correctly predicting one team's score, 1 point for correct winner, draw, or team advanced, and 1 point for correctly guessing which team scores first or that no goal is scored.

The correct one team's score point is awarded only when the full exact score is not correct. For example, if you predict France 2 - 0 Senegal and the actual score is France 2 - 1 Senegal, you get 1 point for correctly predicting France's score.

The Who will win / advance selection is linked to the 1-point correct winner rule. In knockout matches, if the match ends level and one team wins on penalties, the Super Admin can select the team that advanced. Users who selected that team as the winner will receive the winner point.

For example, if you predict France 1 - 1 Senegal, select France as the winner, and France advances after penalties, you can still receive the winner point if the Super Admin selects France as the actual winner.

For a 0-0 match, choose No goal / 0-0 under first team to score. If the match is a knockout match and one team advances after penalties, the Super Admin can still select the advanced team separately.

Bonus prediction points are: 10 points for tournament winner, 10 points for best player, and 5 points for each correct finalist. Bonus predictions can be locked manually by the Super Admin.

You can change your match prediction unlimited times before kickoff. The last saved prediction before the match starts is the one that counts.

Match predictions are auto-locked the moment a match starts — both in the UI and at the database level. Supabase rejects edits after kickoff. Super Admin can reopen a match using override only if needed.

Fixtures are loaded from the local FIFA 2026 portal schedule file. Use the "View on Google" link on any match to open Google's live World Cup card for that fixture.
